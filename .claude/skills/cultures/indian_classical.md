# Indian Classical Music Traditions

## Raga System

A raga (राग) is not simply a scale — it is a melodic framework with:

- **Arohana/Avarohana**: Prescribed ascending and descending patterns (which may differ)
- **Vadi/Samvadi**: Primary and secondary emphasis notes
- **Pakad**: Characteristic melodic phrase that identifies the raga
- **Rasa**: Associated emotional mood (from Bharata's Natyashastra)
- **Time assignment**: Many ragas are traditionally associated with specific times of day

## Key Ragas

| Raga | Time | Mood | Characteristic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Yaman | Evening | Serene, devotional | Sharp 4th (Lydian-like) |
| Bhairav | Dawn | Solemn | Flat 2nd, flat 6th |
| Darbari | Late night | Majestic, contemplative | Microtonal 3rd and 7th |
| Marwa | Sunset | Restless, yearning | No 5th degree |
| Todi | Late morning | Pathos, devotion | Multiple flats + sharp 4th |

## Rhythm (Tala)

- **Tala (ताल)**: Rhythmic cycle defined by a fixed number of beats and subdivision patterns
- **Tintal**: 16 beats (4+4+4+4), the most common
- **Sam (सम)**: The first beat of the cycle — point of resolution
- **Khali (खा��ी)**: "Empty" beat, typically in the third quarter, where the left hand is silent

## Microtonal Elements

Indian classical music uses shrutis (श्रुति) — 22 microtonal intervals per octave. The 12-EDO approximations in YaO capture the scale structure but lose the precise intonation. Raga Darbari's flat 3rd (~280 cents) and flat 7th (~980 cents) are examples of pitches that fall between Western semitones.

## Counter-Examples

- Bollywood film music typically uses Western harmony with Indian melodic ornaments
- Indian fusion (e.g., Shakti) blends raga with jazz harmony
- Carnatic (South Indian) system uses the same pitch material but different theory and nomenclature

## Forbidden Patterns

- Do not treat ragas as "just scales" — the ascending/descending patterns and emphasis notes are essential
- Do not use random notes from a raga scale — respect the pakad and vadi
- Do not assume all Indian music is slow and meditative — many ragas are fast and rhythmically complex
