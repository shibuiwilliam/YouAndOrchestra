# Jazz Ballad — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 60-80 BPM
- Sweet spot: 65-72 BPM (slow enough for rubato, fast enough for momentum)
- Time feel: relaxed swing, triplet-based subdivision
- Rubato is expected, especially in intros and endings
- Drummer plays with brushes, not sticks

## Key Preferences
- D minor (warm, standard jazz minor), C minor (darker, richer)
- F major (bright ballad), Bb major (classic jazz center)
- Frequent modulation via ii-V-I chains to distant keys
- Dorian mode for modal jazz ballad sections
- Mixolydian for blues-inflected passages

## Iconic Chord Progressions (frequency-ranked)
1. ii7-V7-Imaj7 (foundational jazz cadence, ~35%)
2. iiO7-V7b9-i7 (minor ii-V-i, ~20%)
3. Imaj7-vi7-ii7-V7 (rhythm changes turnaround, ~15%)
4. I7-IV7-I7 (blues form adapted, ~10%)
5. Imaj7-bVII7-bVI7-V7 (chromatic descent, ~10%)
6. iii7-VI7-ii7-V7 (extended turnaround, ~5%)

## Drum Pattern Family
- Default: jazz_swing_ride
- Ride cymbal: swing pattern (dotted-eighth + sixteenth feel)
- Hi-hat: foot pedal on 2 and 4
- Kick: sparse, feathered (ghost notes, not driving)
- Snare: brush sweeps and light accents
- No crash cymbals except at section climaxes

## Instrumentation Defaults
- Core: piano (comping and solo), acoustic_bass (walking or pedal), trumpet (melody and solo)
- Common additions: tenor_sax (alternate soloist), vibraphone (textural color)
- Alto_sax for brighter timbral alternative
- Avoid: synths of any kind, electric bass, distorted instruments

## Section Structure
- Intro: 4-8 bars, rubato piano or solo instrument, setting the mood
- Head (A): 8-16 bars, melody stated by trumpet or sax over rhythm section
- Head (B/Bridge): 8 bars, contrasting harmonic area (often up a 4th)
- Solo chorus: 16-32 bars, improvised solo over the form (piano or horn)
- Head out: 8-16 bars, return of melody, possibly reharmonized
- Coda/Tag: 4-8 bars, ritardando, final chord with extensions (maj9, 13)

## Trajectory Patterns
- Classic Ballad: tension 0.2 -> 0.4 -> 0.6 -> 0.7 -> 0.4 -> 0.2
- Late Night: tension 0.15 -> 0.3 -> 0.5 -> 0.3 -> 0.15
- Emotional Peak: tension 0.2 -> 0.3 -> 0.5 -> 0.85 -> 0.3 -> 0.1

## Cadences
- ii7-V7-Imaj7 for standard phrase endings
- Tritone substitution: ii7-bII7-Imaj7 for sophisticated resolution
- Deceptive: ii7-V7-vi7 to extend phrases
- Tag ending: repeat final ii-V-I with ritardando
- Unresolved V7 for open-ended, contemplative endings

## Cliches to AVOID
- Power chords or triads without extensions (jazz requires 7ths minimum)
- Straight eighth-note rhythms (swing is non-negotiable)
- Loud, driving drum patterns (brushes and subtlety are essential)
- Simple root-position voicings (use rootless voicings, shell voicings)
- Resolving every phrase (leave some tension hanging)
- Predictable 4-bar phrasing exclusively (vary phrase lengths)

## Quality Heuristics
- Chord voicings: 7ths and 9ths are standard; 11ths and 13ths add color
- Piano comping: irregular rhythmic placement, not on every beat
- Bass: mostly half notes and quarter notes, chromatic approach tones
- Melody: behind the beat feel, grace notes, blue notes (b3, b7)
- Velocity: soft overall (40-80 range), with subtle dynamic swells
- Swing ratio: 0.65-0.70 (not mechanical triplets, not straight)
- Voice leading: smooth, stepwise movement between chord tones
- Space: rests are as important as notes; do not fill every beat
