# EDM House — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 120-130 BPM (strict — house tempo is defined by this range)
- Sweet spot: 124-128 BPM
- Below 120 = deep house territory; above 130 = tech house/techno

## Key Preferences
- Minor keys dominate (Am, Cm, Fm for emotional resonance)
- Major keys for uplifting/progressive subgenres (C, F, G)
- Key changes rare within a track

## Iconic Chord Progressions
1. vi-IV-I-V (the EDM anthem progression, ~35%)
2. i-VI-III-VII (minor epic, ~25%)
3. i-iv-VI-V (emotional minor, ~20%)
4. I-V-vi-IV (major uplift, ~20%)

## Drum Pattern
- Four-on-the-floor kick: every beat
- Offbeat hi-hat: between every kick
- Clap/snare on 2 and 4
- Sidechain compression ducking on every kick

## Structure (DJ-friendly)
- Intro: 16-32 bars, drums only, gradual element addition
- Buildup: 8-16 bars, rising tension, filter sweeps, risers
- Drop: 16-32 bars, full energy, main hook/synth
- Breakdown: 8-16 bars, stripped back, melodic focus
- Second buildup + drop: repeat with variations
- Outro: 16-32 bars, mirror of intro for DJ mixing

## Instrumentation
- Core: kick drum, hi-hat, clap, synth bass, synth pad/lead
- Common: piano chords, vocal chops, risers/impacts
- Avoid: acoustic instruments (unless as a stylistic choice), orchestral

## Cliches to AVOID
- Generic supersaw buildup without harmonic interest
- Drop that merely repeats the buildup progression at higher volume
- Overcompressed master (house needs groove, not just loudness)
- Snare rolls as the only buildup device
