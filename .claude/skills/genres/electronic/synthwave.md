# Synthwave — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 80-120 BPM
- Sweet spot: 100-110 BPM (driving, nostalgic)
- Slower: 80-90 BPM (dreamwave, atmospheric)
- Faster: 110-120 BPM (outrun, energetic)

## Key Preferences
- Minor keys almost exclusively (Am, Em, Dm, Cm)
- Dorian mode for a slightly brighter minor feel
- Phrygian for darker, more aggressive tracks

## Iconic Chord Progressions
1. i-VI-III-VII (minor anthem, ~35%)
2. i-iv-VI-VII (melancholic drive, ~25%)
3. i-v-VI-IV (minor with dominant pull, ~20%)
4. i-III-VII-VI (descending minor, ~20%)

## Sound Palette
- Analog-style synthesizers: sawtooth leads, warm pads
- Chorus and detuned unison for "lush" character
- Gated reverb on snare drum
- Arpeggiated sequences (16th or 8th notes)
- Deep sub-bass or FM bass

## Drum Pattern
- Kick on 1 and 3, snare on 2 and 4 (standard rock-derived)
- Gated reverb on snare is signature
- Hi-hat: straight eighths or sixteenths
- Tom fills with heavy reverb

## Section Structure
- Intro: 8-16 bars, atmospheric pad + arpeggio fade-in
- Verse: 8-16 bars, bass and drums enter, driving rhythm
- Chorus: 8 bars, full synth lead, peak energy
- Bridge: 8 bars, breakdown, filtered pads
- Outro: 8-16 bars, gradual element removal

## Cliches to AVOID
- Pure 1980s pastiche (synthwave should feel both retro and modern)
- Gated reverb on every element (use selectively on snare/toms)
- Static arpeggiator patterns that never develop
- Ignoring low-end management (sub-bass must be controlled)
