# Game BGM (RPG) — Genre Skill

## Purpose
RPG background music creates atmosphere for extended play sessions (50-100+ hours). It must loop seamlessly, avoid listener fatigue, and support multiple gameplay states.

## Modes
- **Exploration/Town**: Gentle, memorable, loopable. Piano, harp, strings.
- **Dungeon**: Mysterious, tense, lower register. Minor keys, sparse texture.
- **Battle**: Energetic, driving, higher tempo. Full orchestra, prominent rhythm.
- **Boss Battle**: Epic, dramatic, layered intensity.
- **Cutscene**: Narrative, thematic, non-looping.

## Looping
- Loops must be seamless (no audible seam)
- Typical loop: 1-3 minutes
- End of loop should transition smoothly to beginning
- Dynamic range should be consistent across the loop

## Memorable Themes
- Town themes are the most remembered (player hears them most)
- Simple, singable melody is more valuable than complexity
- Theme should be recognizable in 4-8 bars

## Fatigue Prevention
- Avoid excessive repetition of short motifs
- Use variation within loops (subtle changes each cycle)
- Keep dynamics moderate (loud music fatigues faster)
- Leave frequency space for sound effects

## Tempo Guidelines
- Town/exploration: 80-100 BPM
- Dungeon: 60-80 BPM (slower = more tension)
- Battle: 120-140 BPM
- Overworld: 90-110 BPM

## Cliches to AVOID
- 8-bar loops that become obvious after 2 minutes
- Battle music that drowns out sound effects
- Town themes that are too complex to remember
- Jarring transitions between loop iterations
