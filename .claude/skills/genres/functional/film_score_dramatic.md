# Film Score Dramatic — Genre Skill

## Purpose
Dramatic film scoring supports narrative emotion: tension, release, triumph, loss. The music must serve the story, not dominate it.

## Tempo
- Wide range: 40 BPM (adagio suspense) to 160 BPM (action chase)
- Tempo changes are structural tools tied to scene pacing
- Rubato for emotional passages

## Orchestration
- Full symphony orchestra is the default palette
- Strings: emotional core (sustained, tremolo, pizzicato)
- Brass: heroic themes, dramatic punctuation
- Woodwinds: pastoral, intimate, mysterious
- Percussion: timpani, cymbals (sparingly), no drum kit

## Leitmotif
- Central technique: recurring themes associated with characters/ideas
- Theme must be recognizable in varied orchestrations
- Fragmentation and development over the film's arc

## Dynamic Arc
- Music follows the scene's emotional trajectory
- Climax typically at 2/3 mark (golden section)
- Subito piano for dramatic contrast
- Full dynamic range: ppp to fff

## Cliches to AVOID
- Temp-track imitation (compose original themes)
- Constant fortissimo (dynamic range is essential)
- Trailer-style "braams" in every dramatic moment
- Mickey-mousing (over-literal sync with action)
