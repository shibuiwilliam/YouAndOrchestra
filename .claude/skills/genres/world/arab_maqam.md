# Arabic Maqam — Genre Skill

## Cultural Background
The maqam system is the modal foundation of classical and folk music across the Arab world. It predates Western tonal harmony and operates on fundamentally different principles: melody is primary, and intervals include quarter-tones (approximately 50 cents) not found in 12-EDO.

## Maqam System
- A maqam defines a set of pitches, characteristic phrases, and emotional associations
- Maqam Rast: fundamental maqam, joyful/open character, quarter-tone on 3rd and 7th degrees
- Maqam Bayati: warm/earthy, quarter-flat 2nd degree (~150 cents)
- Maqamat are built from tetrachords (jins/ajnas) that combine to form the full scale

## Quarter-Tones
- The 3rd degree of Rast is approximately 350 cents (between E and Eb in C)
- These pitches CANNOT be represented in standard 12-EDO
- Requires MPE MIDI or pitch-bend rendering for authenticity

## Rhythm
- Maqsum: 4/4 pattern (D-T-D-T-T) — most common
- Saidi: 4/4 (DD-T-D-T) — Upper Egyptian
- Masmoudi: 8/4 (D-D--T-D-T) — large form
- "D" = dum (bass), "T" = tak (treble) on darbuka

## Taqasim (Improvisation)
- Solo melodic improvisation over a drone or rhythmic cycle
- Explores the maqam systematically, ascending through registers
- Modulation to related maqamat is a sign of mastery

## Instrumentation
- Melody: oud (fretless lute), nay (end-blown flute), violin (retuned)
- Harmony: qanun (plucked zither — provides heterophonic texture, not chords)
- Rhythm: riq (tambourine), darbuka (goblet drum)

## CRITICAL: What NOT to Do
- Do NOT use equal temperament for quarter-tone maqamat
- Do NOT impose chord progressions — maqam is modal, not tonal
- Do NOT conflate Arabic and Turkish systems (they differ in tuning and theory)
- Do NOT use Western drum kit — use darbuka/riq patterns
