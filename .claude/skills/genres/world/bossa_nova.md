# Bossa Nova — Genre Skill

## Cultural Background
Bossa nova emerged in Rio de Janeiro in the late 1950s as a sophisticated fusion of Brazilian samba rhythm with cool jazz harmony. It represents a uniquely Brazilian artistic achievement and should not be reduced to simply "Brazilian jazz" — it has its own distinct identity.

## The Rhythm
- Based on the samba clave but softer and more intimate
- Guitar pattern: syncopated right-hand pattern (the "bossa beat")
- Bass: syncopated, often anticipating beat 1
- Drums: brushes, light touch, NO heavy backbeat

## Harmony
- Rich jazz harmony: maj7, min7, dom7, altered dominants
- Chromatic bass movement common
- bII (Neapolitan) substitutions
- Voice leading is paramount — smooth motion between chords

## Instrumentation
- Core: nylon-string acoustic guitar (the defining instrument)
- Common: piano, acoustic bass, flute, light drums
- Vocal style: intimate, conversational, close-mic
- Avoid: electric guitar, heavy drums, orchestral bombast

## Tempo
- 110-140 BPM (moderate, relaxed)
- Never rushed — the laid-back feel is essential
- Slightly behind the beat for that characteristic "lazy" groove

## Structure
- Verse-chorus with through-composed tendencies
- Shorter than typical pop (2-3 minutes)
- Instrumental introductions common
- Endings often fade or resolve gently

## Cliches to AVOID
- Straight eighth notes (must have the bossa syncopation)
- Heavy backbeat snare (too aggressive)
- Overly busy arrangements (bossa nova values space)
- Treating it as "background music" (it is art music)
