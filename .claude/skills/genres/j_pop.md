# J-Pop — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 110-135 BPM
- Sweet spot: 120-128 BPM (energetic but singable)
- Ballads may dip to 70-90 BPM but use the ballad_brushed pattern instead
- Uptempo dance-pop variants can reach 140 BPM

## Key Preferences
- C major (bright, standard), G major (open feel), F major (warm), D major (bright energy)
- Key changes up a half or whole step at final chorus are iconic
- Minor keys (Am, Em) for emotional verses that resolve to relative major in chorus
- Frequent use of borrowed chords from parallel minor

## Iconic Chord Progressions (frequency-ranked)
1. IV-V-iii-vi ("royal road" / oji-sama, ~35%)
2. IV-V-I (simple resolution, ~20%)
3. vi-IV-V-I (reverse royal road, ~15%)
4. I-V-vi-iii-IV-I-IV-V (canon progression, ~10%)
5. IVmaj7-V7-iii7-vi7 (jazzy J-pop variant, ~10%)
6. bVI-bVII-I (borrowed chord lift for climactic moments, ~5%)

## Drum Pattern Family
- Default: pop_8beat
- Kick on 1 and 3, snare on 2 and 4
- Hi-hat: straight 8ths, open hat on upbeat of 4
- Fills at end of 4- or 8-bar phrases (tom descents are standard)
- Chorus often adds crash cymbal on beat 1

## Instrumentation Defaults
- Core: piano, acoustic_guitar_steel, electric_bass_finger
- Common additions: strings_ensemble (chorus pads), electric_piano
- Lead melody often doubled by piano and vocal (piano carries it in instrumental)
- Avoid: heavy distortion instruments, brass-heavy arrangements, harpsichord

## Section Structure
- Intro: 4-8 bars, instrumental hook or atmospheric setup
- A-melo (verse): 8-16 bars, lower register, sparse arrangement
- B-melo (pre-chorus): 4-8 bars, builds tension, often modulates
- Sabi (chorus): 8-16 bars, highest energy, full arrangement, memorable hook
- Interlude: 4-8 bars, instrumental bridge
- C-melo (bridge): 4-8 bars, contrasting material before final sabi
- Outro/Last sabi: key change up, maximum emotional intensity

## Trajectory Patterns
- Standard Arc: tension 0.3 -> 0.5 -> 0.8 -> 0.4 -> 0.9 -> 0.3
- Ballad: tension 0.2 -> 0.4 -> 0.7 -> 0.3 -> 0.8 -> 0.2
- Genki (energetic): tension 0.5 -> 0.6 -> 0.9 -> 0.5 -> 0.95 -> 0.4

## Cadences
- Authentic (V-I) for strong chorus endings
- IV-I (plagal) for gentle phrase endings in verses
- bVI-bVII-I for dramatic chorus arrivals (borrowed chord cadence)
- Deceptive (V-vi) at end of B-melo to delay resolution to sabi

## Cliches to AVOID
- Monotonous verse-chorus with no B-melo (the pre-chorus is essential)
- Ignoring the key change convention at final chorus (not required but expected)
- Overly simple bass lines (J-pop bass is melodically active)
- Static dynamics between A-melo and sabi (contrast is critical)
- Excessive syncopation in melody (J-pop melodies are rhythmically clear)

## Quality Heuristics
- Melody should be singable with a range of roughly one octave
- Clear dynamic contrast between verse (mp) and chorus (f)
- Piano voicings: broken chords and arpeggios in verses, block chords in chorus
- Bass: root motion with passing tones, octave jumps at section transitions
- Strings: sustained pads in chorus, absent or minimal in verse
- Arrangement density should clearly differentiate each section

## Use Cases
- Anime opening/ending themes
- Japanese drama soundtracks
- Idol group instrumentals
- Karaoke backing tracks
