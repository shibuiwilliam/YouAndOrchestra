# Orchestral Classical — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 60-160 BPM (varies dramatically by movement/section)
- Adagio: 60-72 BPM (slow movements, expressive)
- Andante: 76-108 BPM (walking pace)
- Allegro: 120-156 BPM (fast, energetic)
- Tempo rubato expected in lyrical passages
- Accelerando and ritardando at phrase boundaries

## Key Preferences
- Sharp keys (G, D, A) for brightness and brilliance
- Flat keys (F, Bb, Eb) for warmth and depth
- C major for grandeur and simplicity
- Minor keys for drama (Dm, Gm, Cm)
- Modulation through related keys (dominant, relative minor) expected
- Remote key relationships for dramatic effect (chromatic mediant)

## Iconic Chord Progressions (frequency-ranked)
1. I-IV-V-I (fundamental cadential motion, ~20%)
2. I-vi-IV-V-I (extended cadence, ~15%)
3. i-iv-V-i (minor cadence with raised leading tone, ~15%)
4. I-V/V-V-I (secondary dominant chain, ~10%)
5. I-bVI-bVII-I (Romantic-era chromatic mediant, ~10%)
6. i-bVI-bIII-bVII (minor descending, ~10%)
7. Sequential patterns: I-IV, V-I, vi-ii, III-vi (circle of fifths, ~10%)

## Drum Pattern Family
- Default: null (no percussion in most orchestral works)
- Timpani for rhythmic punctuation on strong beats (tonic and dominant pitches)
- Cymbals and triangle for climactic moments only
- Snare drum for military or march-like passages
- Percussion is never metronomic — always musically motivated

## Instrumentation Defaults
- Core: strings_ensemble (foundation: violins I & II, violas, cellos, basses)
- Woodwinds: flute, oboe, clarinet, bassoon (color and melody)
- Brass: french_horn (harmonic glue), trumpet (fanfares, climax), trombone (power)
- Keyboard: piano for concerto; not standard in pure orchestral
- Harp for arpeggiated textures and color
- Tutti vs. soli contrast is essential

## Section Structure
- Sonata form: Exposition (theme A + B) → Development → Recapitulation → Coda
- Rondo: A-B-A-C-A or similar
- Theme and Variations: Theme → Var.1 → Var.2 → ... → Finale
- Movements typically: fast–slow–dance–fast (symphony) or fast–slow–fast (concerto)
- Section lengths: 16-64 bars per theme/section
- Development sections feature fragmentation, modulation, counterpoint

## Melody Conventions
- Long-breathed phrases (4-8 bar phrase lengths common)
- Antecedent-consequent phrase pairs
- Wide range usage (2+ octaves across an orchestral melody)
- Stepwise motion with expressive leaps at climactic points
- Motif development: augmentation, diminution, inversion, retrograde, sequence
- Countermelody and imitative counterpoint between sections

## Harmonic Conventions
- Functional harmony with clear cadences (PAC, HC, DC, PC)
- Secondary dominants and applied chords
- Modulation to closely related keys (dominant, relative, parallel)
- Romantic era: chromatic mediants, augmented sixths, Neapolitan
- Suspensions and appoggiaturas for expressive dissonance
- Pedal points (tonic and dominant) for structural anchoring
- Voice leading must be smooth; parallel fifths/octaves forbidden

## Adversarial Critic Rules
- Deal-breaker: parallel fifths or octaves in voice leading
- Deal-breaker: strings written outside playable range
- Cliche: overuse of I-V-I without development → flag
- Warning: all instruments playing simultaneously for >8 bars (orchestration should breathe)
- Warning: no dynamic contrast between sections
- Note: predictability_max is lower than pop — classical rewards surprise and development

## Compatible Genre Blends
- Film score (preserves orchestration, adds programmatic intent)
- Neoclassical (adds minimalist repetition, reduces development)
- Romantic (intensifies chromaticism and emotional expression)
