# Romantic — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Extremely wide range: 40 BPM (largo) to 160 BPM (presto)
- Rubato is a defining characteristic: tempo flexes with emotion
- Accelerando and ritardando are structural tools, not just decoration

## Key Preferences
- Remote keys with many accidentals: Db, Ab, E, B (exotic color)
- F minor, C minor (Beethoven tradition)
- Enharmonic modulation between distant keys is a hallmark

## Iconic Chord Progressions
1. I-vi-ii-V (functional harmony with richness, ~25%)
2. i-VI-iv-V (minor with Neapolitan flavor, ~20%)
3. I-V/vi-vi-IV (secondary dominant usage, ~20%)
4. i-bVI-bIII-V (chromatic mediant relationships, ~15%)
5. Augmented 6th → V → I (dramatic cadence, ~10%)
6. Wagner-style chromatic sequences (~10%)

## Harmonic Language
- Chromatic harmony: secondary dominants, borrowed chords, augmented sixths
- Enharmonic modulation for surprise key changes
- Pedal points for tension building (dominant or tonic)
- Diminished 7th chords as pivot points between keys
- Appoggiaturas and suspensions for emotional weight

## Dynamics
- Full spectrum: ppp to fff
- Crescendo and decrescendo are primary expressive tools
- Sudden dynamic contrasts (sforzando, subito piano)
- Dynamic climax is architecturally planned (often 2/3 through)

## Instrumentation
- Core: piano (solo or with orchestra), strings
- Orchestra: full symphonic forces available
- Solo instruments: violin, cello, french horn (heroic)
- Avoid: harpsichord (anachronistic), electronic instruments

## Texture
- Homophonic with rich accompaniment (arpeggiated piano)
- Melody-dominated but with contrapuntal passages
- Orchestral tutti for climactic moments
- Solo passages for intimate expression

## Section Structure
- Sonata form: Exposition-Development-Recapitulation
- Theme and variations
- Character pieces (short, mood-based forms)
- Through-composed structures following narrative arc

## Cliches to AVOID
- Excessive rubato that loses all sense of pulse
- Over-orchestrating every passage (contrast requires restraint)
- Unresolved dissonance without dramatic justification
- Ignoring voice-leading in chromatic passages
