# Jazz Swing — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 120-220 BPM (medium swing to uptempo)
- Sweet spot: 140-160 BPM (classic swing feel)
- Ballads are a separate subgenre (see jazz_ballad)

## Key Preferences
- Bb, F, Eb, Ab (horn-friendly flat keys)
- C for piano-centric arrangements
- Frequent modulation via ii-V chains

## Iconic Chord Progressions
1. ii7-V7-Imaj7 (the fundamental jazz cadence, ~40%)
2. Imaj7-vi7-ii7-V7 (turnaround, ~25%)
3. I7-IV7-I7-V7 (jazz blues, ~20%)
4. iii7-vi7-ii7-V7 (extended turnaround, ~15%)

## Swing Feel
- Default swing ratio ~60% (long-short eighth note feel)
- Ride cymbal maintains quarter-note pulse with swing pattern
- Walking bass on every beat (quarter notes, chromatic passing tones)

## Instrumentation
- Core: piano (comping), acoustic bass (walking), drum kit (brushes or sticks)
- Common: saxophone (alto/tenor), trumpet, trombone
- Avoid: distorted guitar, synths, orchestral percussion

## Voicing Guidelines
- Minimum 7th chords (never bare triads in jazz context)
- Common extensions: 9th, 11th, 13th
- Drop-2 and rootless voicings for piano comping
- Voice leading: minimize movement between chord tones

## Section Structure
- Head: 32 bars (AABA form common), melody statement
- Solos: 1+ choruses per soloist over the form
- Shout chorus: arranged ensemble passage (optional)
- Head out: return to melody, often with coda

## Cliches to AVOID
- Playing straight eighths (must swing)
- Using simple triads (7th chords minimum)
- Static bass (must walk)
- Ignoring the form during solos
