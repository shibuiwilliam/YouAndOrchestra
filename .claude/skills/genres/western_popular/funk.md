# Funk — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 90-130 BPM
- Sweet spot: 100-110 BPM (deep pocket groove)
- Faster funk: 120-130 BPM (dance-oriented)

## Key Preferences
- Single-chord or two-chord vamps dominate
- Guitar-friendly: E, A, D (open strings for muting)
- Horn-friendly: Bb, Eb for horn section arrangements

## Iconic Chord Progressions
1. I7 vamp (one-chord funk, ~40%)
2. i7-IV7 (two-chord groove, ~25%)
3. I9-IV9 (extension-rich vamp, ~20%)
4. i7-bVII7 (modal funk, ~15%)

## The Groove
- Sixteenth-note subdivision is the heartbeat of funk
- "The One" — heavy emphasis on beat 1 of every phrase
- Ghost notes on snare fill the space between accents
- Bass and drums MUST lock together (the "pocket")
- Slight behind-the-beat feel for deep groove

## Instrumentation
- Core: electric bass (fingerstyle or slap), drums, rhythm guitar
- Common: clavinet, horns (trumpet, trombone, sax), organ
- Avoid: orchestral instruments, heavy reverb, distortion

## Bass Guidelines
- Syncopated sixteenth-note patterns
- Slap technique for percussive accents
- Octave jumps and chromatic approach notes
- Lock with kick drum pattern

## Section Structure
- Funk is groove-driven, not form-driven
- Intro: 4-8 bars establishing the groove
- Verse: 8-16 bars, groove continues, vocals ride on top
- Chorus/hook: 4-8 bars, slight harmonic shift or dynamic lift
- Bridge: 4 bars, breakdown or stop-time hits
- Breakdown: drums and bass only, building back up

## Cliches to AVOID
- Overplaying (funk is about space and restraint)
- Losing the pocket by rushing
- Too many chord changes (2 chords is often enough)
- Ignoring ghost notes (they define the texture)
- Heavy reverb (funk is dry and tight)
