# Blues — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 60-140 BPM
- Slow blues: 60-80 BPM (expressive, rubato-like)
- Medium shuffle: 100-120 BPM (classic groove)
- Uptempo: 120-140 BPM (boogie/jump blues)

## Key Preferences
- Guitar-friendly: E, A, G, D (open string resonance)
- Piano blues: C, F, Bb
- Minor blues: Am, Em, Dm

## Iconic Chord Progressions
1. 12-bar blues: I7-I7-I7-I7 | IV7-IV7-I7-I7 | V7-IV7-I7-V7 (~60%)
2. 8-bar blues: I7-V7-IV7-IV7 | I7-V7-I7-I7 (~15%)
3. Minor blues: i7-iv7-i7-V7 (~15%)
4. Quick-change: I7-IV7-I7-I7 | IV7-IV7-I7-I7 | V7-IV7-I7-V7 (~10%)

## Blue Notes and Scales
- Blues scale: 1-b3-4-b5-5-b7
- b3, b5, b7 are the defining "blue notes"
- Mix major and minor thirds for tension

## Rhythm
- Shuffle feel: triplet-based swing (long-short-long-short)
- Slow blues: triplet eighth notes with rubato phrasing
- Boogie-woogie: steady eighth-note left hand pattern

## Instrumentation
- Core: guitar (clean or light overdrive), bass, drums
- Common: piano, harmonica, horns (in horn sections)
- Avoid: orchestral instruments, synthesizers, heavy distortion

## Section Structure
- Blues is form-driven: the 12-bar form IS the structure
- Verses repeat over the form with lyrical/melodic variation
- Solos follow the same 12-bar form
- Turnaround (last 2 bars) signals return to top

## Cliches to AVOID
- Exact repetition of 12-bar form without melodic development
- Forgetting the shuffle feel (straight eighths kill the groove)
- Overusing pentatonic cliches without chromatic passing tones
- Ignoring dynamics (blues is highly expressive)
