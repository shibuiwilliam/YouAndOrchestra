# Pop — Genre Skill

## Tempo
- Range: 100-130 BPM
- Sweet spot: 110-120 BPM for mid-tempo, 120-128 for uptempo
- Steady pulse essential; tempo changes rare except for dramatic endings
- BPM consistency within ±2 throughout the track

## Key Preferences
- C major / G major / D major (bright, accessible)
- A major / F major (warm, radio-friendly)
- Am / Em for minor-key pop
- Key changes (up a semitone or whole tone) at final chorus are a genre convention

## Iconic Chord Progressions (frequency-ranked)
1. I-V-vi-IV (the "pop progression", ~30%)
2. vi-IV-I-V (emotional variant, ~20%)
3. I-IV-vi-V (open feel, ~15%)
4. I-vi-IV-V (classic 50s/doo-wop, ~10%)
5. I-V-IV-V (anthem feel, ~10%)
6. vi-I-V-IV (minor start, ~10%)

## Drum Pattern Family
- Default: pop_straight (four-on-floor or backbeat)
- Kick on 1 and 3 (or four-on-floor), snare on 2 and 4
- Hi-hat 8ths or 16ths, open hi-hat on upbeats for energy
- Builds use toms; drops may remove kick briefly
- Fills at 4- and 8-bar boundaries

## Instrumentation Defaults
- Core: piano or acoustic_guitar (harmonic bed), electric_bass_finger (root-fifth patterns), drums
- Melody: vocal (primary), synth or piano (secondary hooks)
- Strings: strings_ensemble for chorus lifts
- Synth pads for atmosphere in verses
- Avoid: heavy distortion, complex counterpoint, free jazz voicings

## Section Structure
- Intro: 4-8 bars (hook tease or stripped-down groove)
- Verse: 8-16 bars (lower energy, storytelling)
- Pre-chorus: 4-8 bars (build tension, rising melody)
- Chorus: 8-16 bars (highest energy, hook repetition)
- Bridge: 4-8 bars (contrast, new harmonic territory)
- Outro: 4-8 bars (fade or final hook repetition)
- Total: 3-4 minutes (180-240 bars at ~120 BPM)

## Melody Conventions
- Singable range: typically within one octave (C4-C5)
- Strong hook within first 30 seconds
- Stepwise motion in verses, wider intervals in choruses
- Repetition of melodic hooks is a virtue, not a flaw
- Pentatonic tendencies in hooks for memorability

## Harmonic Conventions
- Diatonic harmony dominant; chromatic mediants for surprise
- Pre-chorus often moves to IV or ii as dominant preparation
- Chorus harmony should feel "arrived" — root position, strong bass
- Bridge: explore vi, iii, or borrow from parallel minor

## Adversarial Critic Rules
- Cliche: I-V-vi-IV used in every section without variation → flag
- Deal-breaker: no identifiable hook after 30 seconds
- Deal-breaker: section contrast below threshold (verse must differ from chorus)
- Warning: melody range exceeding two octaves (unsignable)
